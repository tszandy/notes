Most commercial websites today are hosted on one of two "tiers," depending on the size of the company and the complexity of the site. 

In 2026, the landscape is dominated by three main categories:

### 1. The "Big Three" Cloud Giants (Enterprise & Tech)
About **80% of the global cloud infrastructure** is controlled by three massive providers. Most major apps (like Netflix, Airbnb, or Uber) live here.
* **Amazon Web Services (AWS):** The market leader. It powers roughly **5.1% of all websites globally** and is the go-to for complex, high-traffic platforms.
* **Microsoft Azure:** Used heavily by large corporations that already use Microsoft software. It has seen massive growth recently due to its integration with enterprise AI tools.
* **Google Cloud (GCP):** Often chosen by tech-heavy startups and companies focused on data analytics and high-performance networking.

### 2. Specialized "Developer Platforms" (SaaS & Modern Web)
For modern full-stack sites (like the one you're writing in **Go and React**), many companies have moved away from the "Big Three" to more specialized platforms that automate the hard work.
* **Vercel / Netlify:** These dominate the **React/TypeScript** space. They handle the "frontend" of millions of commercial sites because they are incredibly fast and easy for developers to use.
* **Cloudflare:** While they started with security, Cloudflare now hosts a massive portion of the "top 1 million" websites because they run code at the "edge" (physically close to the user).

### 3. Traditional Shared/Managed Hosting (Small Business)
For the millions of local businesses, blogs, and restaurants, traditional "managed" hosting is still king.
* **WordPress:** Believe it or not, **43.5% of the entire internet** runs on WordPress. 
* **Top Providers:** Companies like **Hostinger, Bluehost, and SiteGround** host the bulk of small-to-medium business sites because they are cheap ($3–$10/month) and come with 24/7 support.

### Summary Table: Where do sites live?

| Website Type | Primary Hosting Environment | Examples |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Giant Apps** | Public Cloud (AWS / Azure) | Netflix, Amazon, Banks |
| **SaaS / Tech Startups** | Developer Platforms (Vercel / Railway) | Linear, Loom, Midjourney |
| **Small Businesses** | Managed Hosting (Hostinger / Bluehost) | Law firms, local shops, blogs |
| **Static / High Traffic** | Edge Networks (Cloudflare) | News sites, Documentation |

**The Trend in 2026:** Most commercial sites are now **"Hybrid."** They might host their **React frontend** on Vercel for speed, but keep their **Go backend** and database on AWS for security and scale. 

Since you're using **Go and Docker**, you are technically in the "SaaS/Tech Startup" category—which means you'll likely feel most at home on a platform like **Railway** or **AWS App Runner** rather than a traditional host like Bluehost.
